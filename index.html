Galatac

Modern Gaulish

Galatac is the ancient Gaulish language, reborn for modern speakers. Though today's Celtic languages survive only in small, endangered communities, Celtic languages once dominated all of West and Central Europe.

If you already speak a Celtic language, you'll spot plenty that feels familiar. If you speak a Germanic or Romance language, you'll recognize scattered words here and there, and Galatac will serve as your introduction to a whole sister branch of the family. A sister who's been missing for a thousand years. Her belongings strewn about the house. You had forgotten her, but she is now standing in the doorway.

Brief Grammar

Word order : Verb-Subject-Object. Ex. : Gena si gnatal (births she baby).

Nouns : Oftem -a bat bu sempre. Ex. : jiva, dwar, fish.

Adjectives : Often -ac / -ic / -in / -id . Ex. : forte, sekret-ney

Adverbs : Often inte + [adj]. Ex. : inte nowi (newly).

Verbs : Verb roots can end on -n, -r, -l, -m, -s, -/g/, -/ge/, -/i/, -/a/, -/wi/, diphthongs on -i, mono-syllabic verbs on -i, and one single verb on /o/. Ex. : men, reti, anwi, gwei, cara.

Present tense : Add or swap -a. Ex. : mena, reta, anwia, gweia, cara .

Present Perfect : [verb root]-tu. Ex. : mentu, retitu, anwitu, gweitu, caratu.

Past tense re + [verb root]. Ex. : re men, re reti, re anwi, re gwei, re cara.

Past Perfect : re + [verb root]-tu. Ex. : re mentu, re retitu, re anwitu, re gweitu, re caratu.

Future tense : [verb root]-si. Ex. : mensi, retisi, anwisi, gweisi, carasi

Conditional mood : re + [verb root]-si. Ex. : re mensi, re retisi, re anwisi, re gweisi, re carasi.

Past Participle : -tu.

Diminutive : -al. Feminine : -is. Plural : -e.

Definite article in. Adjectives invariable.

Personal Pronouns : Mi, ti, (e, si, i) ; Plural : Ni, swi, si.
